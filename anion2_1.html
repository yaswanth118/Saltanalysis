Conformation of Chloride ion.

As per your observation the possible anion might be chloride ion.
But we need to do conformation test for this ion.Now follow the given steps in order to confirm the anion present.
  • Take 0.1 g of salt in a test tube, add a pinch of manganese dioxide and 3-4 drops of conc. sulphuric acid. Heat the reaction mixture. Greenish yellow chlorine gas is evolved which is detected by its pungent odour and bleaching action.
  • Take 1 mL of sodium carbonate extract in a test tube, acidfy it with dil. HNO 3 or take water extract and add silver nitrate solution. A curdy white precipitate is obtained which is soluble in ammonium hydroxide solution.
  • Take 0.1 g salt and a pinch of solid potassium dichromate in a test tube, add conc. H 2 SO 4 , heat and pass the gas evolved through sodium hydroxide solution. It becomes yellow. Divide the solution into two parts. Acidify one part with acetic acid and add lead acetate solution. A yellow precipitate is formed. Acidify the second part with dilute sulphuric acid and add 1 mL of amyl alcohol followed by 1 mL of 10% hydrogen peroxide. After gentle shaking the organic layer turns blue.


Perform any two of the conformation tests given above.
