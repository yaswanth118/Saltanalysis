Chemistry behind this analysis

  1. Effervescence with the evolution of a colourless and odourless gas onadding dil. H2SO4 to the solid salt, this indicates the presence of carbonate ion. The gas turns lime water milky due to the formation of CaCO3

    • Na2CO3+ H2SO4 ---> Na2SO4+ H2O +CO2
    • Ca(OH)2+ CO2 --->CaCO3 + H2O

  2. If CO2 gas is passed in excess through lime water, the milkiness produced disappears due to the formation of calcium hydrogen carbonate which is soluble in water.

    • CaCO3+ CO2+ H2O ---> Ca(HCO3)2
