Conformation of Bromide ion.

As per your observation the possible anion might be bhloride ion.
But we need to do conformation test for this ion.Now follow the given steps in order to confirm the anion present.
  • Take 0.1 g of salt and a pinch of MnO2 in a test tube. Add 3-4 drops conc.sulphuric acid and heat. Intense brown fumes are evolved.
  • Neutralise 1 mL of sodium carbonate extract with hydrochloric acid (or take the water extract). Add 1 mL carbon tetrachloride (CCl4)/chloroform (CHCl3)/ carbon disulphide. Now add an excess of chlorine water dropwise and shake the test tube. A brown colouration in the organic layer confirms the presence of bromide ion.
  • Acidify 1 mL of sodium carbonate extract with dil. HNO3 (or take 1 mL water extract) and add silver nitrate solution. A pale yellow precipitate soluble with difficulty in ammonium hydroxide solution is obtained.
