Chemistry behind this analysis

  1. If on heating the salt with conc. H2SO4 , deep violet vapours with a pungent smell are evolved. These turns starch paper blue and a violet sublimate is formed on the sides of the test tube, it indicates the presence of iodide ions. Some HI, sulphur dioxide, hydrogen sulphide, and sulphur are also formed due to the following reactions.On adding MnO 2 to the reaction mixture, the violet vapours become dense.

    • 2 NaI + 2 H2SO4 ---> Na2SO4 + SO2 + 2 H2O + I2
    • I2 + Starch solution ---> Blue color
    • NaI + H2SO4 ---> NaHSO4 + HI
    • 2 HI + H2SO4 ---> 2 H2O + I2 + SO2
    • 6 NaI + 4 H2SO4 ---> 3 I2 + 4 H2O + S + 3 Na2SO4
    • 8 NaI + 5 H2SO4 ---> 4 I2 + H2S + 4 Na2SO4 + 4 H2O
    • 2 NaI + MnO2 + 2 H2SO4 ---> I2 + MnSO4 + Na2SO4 + 2 H2O

  2. Add 1 mL of CHCl3 or CCl4 and chlorine water in excess to the salt solution in water or sodium carbonate extract neutralised with dil.HCl and shake the test tube vigorously. Presence of violet colouration in the organic layer confirms the presence of iodide ions.Iodine dissolves in the organic solvent and the solution becomes violet.

    • 2 NaI + Cl2 ---> 2 NaCl + I2

  3. Acidify sodium carbonate extract of the salt with dil.HNO3 and add AgNO3 solution. Appearance of a yellow precipitate.

    • NaI + AgNO3 ---> AgI(Yellow ppt) + NaNO3
